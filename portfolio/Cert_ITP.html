Environment/Context

As part of my studies at EPHEC, I had to do an internship in an IT company. Wanting to work in the field of network / cybersecurity, I turned to the ITP company, specializing in the implementation of networks and telephony systems. I discovered this company in Ephec's suggestions for finding an internship location.
I was originally more interested in the network aspect than the VoIP aspect but over the course of my weeks of internship, I was able to learn a lot about the needs of companies in telephony.
I've realised this internship with my partner : Timothy Gilles

What I've learned and how will it serves me

I learned a lot of things while working as an intern for ITP.
Whether it was in relation to the tools used in companies (cable tracer, IP scanner, firewall, etc.) or the way of proceeding when establishing a contract with a new customer (analysis of needs and possible solutions , adaptation to an already existing situation, on-site visit of the infrastructure and location, implementation of the solution, etc.).
In addition to all this, I had the opportunity to learn more about telephony, the needs of the market but also the importance of securing communications via VoIP.
I also learned to use a 3D printing machine in my free time, so I was able to familiarize myself with the CURA software and the printing methods. I made several mistakes at the beginning that made me realize what to watch out for when printing.
And finally, I also learned a lot about the Avaya suite that ITP works with. Including how to configure an IPO (telephone exchange) in a fairly basic way, configure H323 SIP telephones from different brands, how dect telephones work and how to configure their antennas and much more. Everything I learned here will be useful to me later if I have to set up a telephone system for a company or mine but also because I have learned of the existence of a sector in cybersecurity dedicated to SIP telephony. .

Conclusion

I am happy to have chosen ITP for my internship. Not only because I had the opportunity to work in a close-knit and cool team, but also because I learned a lot about subjects that at first glance didn't seem particularly interesting to me.
